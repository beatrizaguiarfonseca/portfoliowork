Designing a platform to reduce college move-out waste by connecting students for secondhand furniture exchange.

Overview

Every spring, Northwestern students dump tons of usable furniture. Seniors need to move out quickly. Rising juniors want furniture—but can't store it. Our goal? Create a circular solution that reduces waste and stress.

How might we reduce college move-out waste by facilitating sustainable and convenient item exchanges between students?

Our team researched the current move-out experience, identified gaps between intentions and actions, and prototyped a digital platform that connects students through verified listings, incentives, and timing-sensitive logistics.

What We Found

  • 230 tons of furniture waste is generated annually at the average U.S. college move-out.
  • 83% of items still go to waste—even with donation options.
  • Students feel overwhelmed by time pressure and emotional stress during move-out.
  • Parents are willing to help, but feel limited by distance, time, and university communication gaps.
  • Sustainability programs like sustainNU are severely underfunded and under-supported.

Brainstorming, Researching, Prototyping & User Testing

From app wireframes to pop-up furniture swaps, we tested multiple directions. Our prototypes explored both digital and physical interactions to reduce barriers during move-out.

Reflections

This project challenged me to think beyond screens and into real-world behavior, habits, and emotions. Our solution wasn’t just about an app—it was about understanding what stands in the way of sustainable action and designing something that helps bridge that gap.
