Overview

As part of Northwestern’s Bay Area Immersion Program hosted by the Segal Design Institute, our team partnered with Amazon Music to explore how physical merchandise could elevate the digital listening experience. We investigated how tangible, shippable products—leveraging Amazon’s logistics—could strengthen music fandom and deepen user engagement.

How might we leverage Amazon’s expertise in physical products to make Amazon Music a more recognizable part of the Amazon brand?

Our final concepts included a Listening Rewards Program, a surprise Advent Box, a fan-driven music room builder, and a virtual listening party. But the standout was the Amazon Music Playback Poster—a personalized, auto-generated print sent to super-listeners who streamed 10,000+ minutes per year.

I designed the poster myself, and it was highlighted as a favorite by the product team.

“I love all of these ideas! I feel there is a lot of potential in this and I'm especially impressed and curious about the yearly recap posters.”
— Ebube, Amazon Music Product Manager

Process

1. Research & Discovery

We kicked off with user interviews, competitive benchmarking, and trend analysis focused on Gen Z listening behaviors. One key insight emerged: listeners don’t just consume music—they express identity through it. They want to showcase what they listen to, and why.

2. Ideation & Exploration

We brainstormed cross-platform features that could blend Amazon’s strengths with music fandom culture. Concepts included:

  • “Design My Music Room” – a decor tool powered by your top artists
  • Virtual Listening Parties for real-time reactions with friends
  • An Amazon Prime-style Advent Calendar with artist-themed surprises

Ultimately, the playback poster concept stood out—simple, personal, and scalable. We designed the visuals, mapped the logistics, and presented the full suite of ideas in person at Amazon Music’s San Francisco headquarters during our final pitch.

Brainstorming & Prototyping

Before jumping into Figma and final visuals, we spent time sketching, discussing, and physically prototyping ideas like the Amazon Advent Calendar. Our team ideated in workshops, sticky notes included.

Reflections

This project taught me how to blend emotional user needs with scalable business solutions. Designing for Amazon's ecosystem required thinking way beyond screens — and into cardboard, ink, nostalgia, and the social psychology of sharing music fandom online.
